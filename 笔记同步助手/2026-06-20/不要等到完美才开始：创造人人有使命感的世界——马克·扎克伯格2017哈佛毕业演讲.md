## 使命感的真正含义

### 肯尼迪与清洁工的故事

他引用肯尼迪参观NASA的故事：总统问清洁工在做什么，清洁工回答“我在帮助把人送上月球”。使命感就是感到自己属于比个人更大的事业。

He cited JFK's visit to NASA: the president asked a janitor what he was doing, and the janitor replied, "I'm helping put a man on the moon." Purpose is feeling part of something bigger than yourself.

![[笔记同步助手/images/7fa5110e8d6862151c1ab174d1c4cc93_MD5.jpg|使命感的故事]]

使命感的故事

使命感意味着你被需要，并且有更好的目标值得努力；它是真正幸福的来源。当下尤其需要使命感：上一代人的使命感常来自工作、教会和社区，但今天技术和自动化正在消除大量工作，社区联系也在衰退。许多人感到疏离、抑郁。他见过少年犯和阿片成瘾者，说如果当时有课后项目或可去之处，人生也许会不同。他也见过工厂工人，明知旧工作不会回来，只能努力寻找未来方向。

Purpose means you are needed and there is something better to work towards; it is the source of true happiness. It's especially needed today: previous generations found purpose in work, church, and community, but technology and automation are eliminating many jobs and community ties. Many feel disconnected and depressed. He met juvenile delinquents and opioid addicts who said if they had had after-school programs or a place to go, their lives might have been different. He also met factory workers who knew their old jobs weren't coming back and had to find new paths.

## 个人教训：没有共同使命的危机

他曾告诉朋友：很高兴帮助连接哈佛社区，但总有一天有人会连接全世界；当时没想过那个人会是自己，因为他们只是大学生，以为大公司会去做。但“人们都想彼此连接”的想法很清晰，所以团队日复一日坚持做。很多改变世界的事看起来应由别人完成，但现实是：不会有人替你做，你要去做。仅自己有使命感不够，还必须为他人创造共同使命感。Facebook成立几年后，大公司提出收购，他不想卖，希望继续连接更多人。几乎所有其他人想出售公司，在缺乏更高使命感时，收购就是他们的创业梦想，导致公司内部严重分裂。一位亲近顾问警告他：如果不同意出售，将后悔一生。随后一年左右，管理团队几乎全部离开。那是他经营Facebook最艰难的时期：相信自己做的事，却感到孤独，并意识到问题出在自己没有建立共同目标。多年后他明白：没有更高使命感时，组织就会这样运转。因此每个人都有责任创造共同目的。

He once told a friend: I'm glad we connected the Harvard community, but someday someone will connect the whole world. At the time he never thought it would be him—they were just college students who assumed big companies would do it. But the idea that people want to connect was clear, so the team kept at it. Many world-changing things seem like someone else's job, but no one will do it for you—you have to do it. Having your own purpose isn't enough; you need to create a shared purpose. A few years in, a big company offered to buy Facebook. He didn't want to sell; he wanted to connect more people. Almost everyone else wanted to sell. Without a higher purpose, the acquisition was their dream exit, causing deep division. A close advisor warned he'd regret it for the rest of his life if he didn't sell. Within a year, most of the management team had left. That was the hardest period: he believed in what he was doing but felt alone, and realized he hadn't built a shared mission. Years later he understood: without a higher purpose, that's how organizations work. So everyone must create common purpose.

## 三大方式之一：承担重大而有意义的项目

创造人人有使命感的世界有三种方式。第一，承担重大而有意义的项目。每一代人都有代表性工程：30多万人参与登月，数百万志愿者为全球儿童接种小儿麻痹症疫苗，数百万人建设胡佛水坝。现在轮到这一代人做伟大的事。开始时没人知道怎样建大坝、怎样组织百万人，想法不是一开始就完整，而是在行动中逐渐清晰。关键是先开始。如果他必须先完全懂得如何连接所有人，才开始行动，就不会有Facebook。电影和流行文化误导了创新：所谓“灵光一现”的顿悟时刻是危险谎言，会让人因等待完美点子而自我怀疑。理想主义是好事，但要准备好被误解。任何追求宏大愿景的人都会被称为疯狂，即使最后被证明正确。社会常因害怕犯错而不敢做大事，却忽视了不作为会让现有问题继续存在。这一代人该启动属于自己的伟大工程：例如阻止气候变化，动员数百万人安装太阳能板；追求治愈所有疾病，通过志愿者分享健康数据和基因组；现代化民主，让所有人能在线投票；个性化教育，让每个人都能学习。做大事不仅为了创造进步，更是为了让社会中的每个人都有角色和使命感。

There are three ways to create a world where everyone has purpose. First, take on big meaningful projects. Every generation has its defining projects: 300,000+ people helped put a man on the moon, millions of volunteers vaccinated children against polio, millions built the Hoover Dam. Now it's this generation's turn. Nobody knew how to build a dam or organize millions at the start. Ideas become clear through action. The key is to start. If he had needed to understand how to connect everyone before acting, there would be no Facebook. Movies and pop culture mislead us: the "eureka moment" is a dangerous lie that makes people doubt themselves while waiting for the perfect idea. Idealism is good, but be prepared to be misunderstood. Anyone pursuing a grand vision will be called crazy, even if proven right. Society often fears mistakes and avoids big endeavors, ignoring that inaction leaves existing problems unsolved. This generation should start its own great projects: stop climate change by mobilizing millions to install solar panels, cure all diseases by having volunteers share health data and genomes, modernize democracy so everyone can vote online, personalize education so everyone can learn. Big projects aren't just about progress—they give everyone a role and a sense of purpose.

## 三大方式之二：重新定义平等

第二步是重新定义平等：让每个人都有追求自己使命的自由。父辈多有稳定职业，而当代人更具创业性，无论是开启自己的项目，还是参与他人的项目。创业文化的繁荣，取决于人们能否轻松尝试大量新想法；成功往往来自多次试错。Facebook不是他做的第一个项目，此前还做过聊天系统、游戏、学习工具和音乐播放器。J.K.罗琳在《哈利·波特》出版前被拒12次；碧昂丝也创作了数百首歌才有《Halo》。伟大的成功来自“允许失败”的自由。当今社会财富与机会不平等伤害所有人；如果人们无法把想法变成事业，整个社会都会失去可能性。现有体系过度奖励已经成功的人，却没有给每个人足够多尝试不同机会的条件。有人十年赚数十亿美元，同时数百万学生连贷款都还不起，更别说创业。很少有人因担心赚得不够多而放弃创业，但太多人因没有失败后的缓冲垫，无法追求梦想。成功不只靠好点子和努力，也依赖运气。如果他小时候必须养家、没有时间学编程，或不知道Facebook失败后仍能过得下去，就不会站在这里。每一代人都会扩大平等的定义：前人争取投票权、民权，推动新政和“伟大社会”。现在轮到这一代定义新的社会契约。社会衡量进步不应只看GDP等经济指标，还应看有多少人拥有有意义的角色。应探索全民基本收入，为每个人提供尝试新想法的安全垫。人们会多次更换工作和角色，因此需要负担得起的儿童照护，以及不绑定单一雇主的医疗保障。每个人都会犯错，社会应减少囚禁和污名化，技术持续变化时也要提供终身教育。让每个人都有追求使命的自由并非免费，像他这样受益最多的人应承担更多成本。他与Priscilla创立Chan Zuckerberg Initiative，并承诺投入财富促进机会平等。这些是整个一代人的价值观，问题从不是要不要做，而是什么时候做。千禧一代已是历史上最慈善的一代之一：一年内超过四分之三美国千禧一代捐款，超过七成曾为他人募款。贡献不只包括金钱，也包括时间；每周花一两个小时帮助别人，就能支持他们发挥潜力。Priscilla从哈佛毕业后当老师，并要求他在参与教育工作前亲自获得课堂教学经验。他在当地Boys and Girls Club教课后项目，教授创业、产品开发和营销。学生也让他了解因种族而被针对、家庭成员遭遇困境等真实成长经历。作者连续五年每月与一群学生共进晚餐、陪伴他们成长，其中一人还为他和Priscilla举办了第一次迎婴派对；这些学生明年都将成为家族第一代大学生。每个人都可以抽出时间帮助他人，让更多人拥有追求目标的自由；这不仅是正确的事，也会让更多梦想转化为成果，使所有人受益。

The second way is to redefine equality—giving everyone the freedom to pursue their purpose. Our parents had stable careers, but this generation is more entrepreneurial, whether starting their own projects or joining others'. An entrepreneurial culture thrives when people can try many ideas; success often comes from trial and error. Facebook wasn't his first project—he built chat systems, games, study tools, and a music player before. J.K. Rowling was rejected 12 times before Harry Potter; Beyoncé wrote hundreds of songs before "Halo". Great success comes from the freedom to fail. Inequality of wealth and opportunity hurts everyone; if people can't turn ideas into ventures, society loses possibilities. The current system over-rewards those who already succeed and doesn't give everyone enough chances to try. Some make billions while millions can't even pay their loans, let alone start a business. Few quit due to fear of not making enough, but many can't pursue dreams without a safety net. Success depends not only on good ideas and hard work but also luck. If he had to support his family as a kid, had no time to learn programming, or didn't know he could survive a Facebook failure, he wouldn't be here. Every generation expands equality: previous ones won voting rights, civil rights, the New Deal, and the Great Society. Now it's this generation's turn to define a new social contract. Progress should be measured not just by GDP but by how many people have meaningful roles. We should explore universal basic income to give everyone a cushion to try new ideas. People will change jobs and roles many times, so we need affordable childcare and health care not tied to a single employer. Everyone makes mistakes; society should reduce incarceration and stigmatization, and provide lifelong learning as technology changes. Giving everyone the freedom to pursue purpose is not free—those who benefit most, like him, should bear more cost. He and Priscilla started the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative and committed their wealth to promote equal opportunity. These are the values of this generation; the question is not whether to do it, but when. Millennials are already the most charitable generation: over three-quarters donated money in a year, and over seven thousand raised funds for others. Contribution isn't just money—it's time. Spending an hour or two a week helping others can support them to reach their potential. Priscilla became a teacher after Harvard and required him to gain classroom experience before getting involved in education. He taught an after-school program at Boys and Girls Club on entrepreneurship, product development, and marketing. Students shared their real struggles—being targeted because of race, family members facing hardship. He had dinner with a group of students every month for five years, watching them grow; one of them even hosted the first baby shower for him and Priscilla. Next year those students will be the first in their families to go to college. Everyone can take time to help others, giving more people the freedom to pursue their goals. It's not only the right thing to do—it will turn more dreams into reality, benefiting everyone.

## 三大方式之三：建设社区

第三种方式是建设社区；这里的“全民”不止一国一地，而是全世界的人。一项针对全球千禧一代的调查显示，最能定义他们身份的答案不是国籍、族裔或宗教，而是“世界公民”。每一代人都会扩大“我们”的范围：从部落到城市、国家，再到今天的全球共同体，人类通过更大规模的连接完成个人无法完成的事。当代最大的机会和挑战都具有全球性：我们可能成为终结贫困和疾病的一代，但气候变化、疫情等问题也需要全球协作应对。当下世界并不稳定，全球化让一些人被落下，若人们在本地生活中缺乏安全感和目标感，就很难关心远方的人。这个时代的核心斗争，是自由、开放、全球共同体，与威权主义、孤立主义、民族主义之间的观念之争；也是知识、贸易、移民流动与阻碍它们的力量之争。这场斗争不会只在联合国或国家层面解决，而会在地方层面发生：当足够多人在自己的生活中获得使命感和稳定感，才会愿意向外开放、关心他人。最有效的做法是从现在开始建设本地社区，因为社区让人感到自己属于更大的整体、不再孤单，并获得拓展视野的力量。过去几十年，各类社区组织的成员数量下降了多达四分之一，许多人因此需要在别处寻找使命感；但社区可以被重建，也可以被重新创造。例子一：毕业生Agnes Segoye童年经历乌干达冲突地区与人口贩卖风险，后来培训数千名执法人员，帮助社区保持安全。例子二：Kaila Oakley和Niha Jain创办了非营利医疗组织，服务社区需求。例子三：David曾任市议员，推动墨西哥城成为拉丁美洲第一个通过婚姻平权的城市，甚至早于旧金山。作者自己的经历也是从宿舍里的一个学生开始，一次连接一个社区，坚持下去，最终推动连接整个世界。即使是全球性改变，也始于小处，始于像我们一样的人；这一代能否实现更大连接和更大机会，取决于我们能否建设社区，让每个人拥有使命感。

The third way is to build community—and "everyone" means the whole world, not just one country. A global survey of millennials found that the top identifier wasn't nationality, ethnicity, or religion, but "citizen of the world." Each generation expands the circle of "us": from tribe to city to nation, now to a global community. Humanity accomplishes through larger connections what individuals cannot. The biggest opportunities and challenges are global: we could be the generation that ends poverty and disease, but climate change, pandemics, etc. require global cooperation. The world is unstable; globalization has left some behind. If people lack security and purpose in their local lives, they can't care about distant others. The central struggle of our time is between forces of freedom, openness, and global community versus authoritarianism, isolationism, and nationalism—between knowledge, trade, immigration and those that block them. This battle won't be solved only at the UN or national level, but at the local level: when enough people find purpose and stability in their own lives, they will be willing to open up and care about others. The most effective thing is to start building local communities now, because communities make us feel part of something bigger, less alone, and give us strength to reach out. Over the past few decades, membership in community organizations has dropped by as much as a quarter, so many seek purpose elsewhere; but communities can be rebuilt and recreated. Example: graduate Agnes Segoye grew up in a conflict zone in Uganda and faced trafficking risks; she later trained thousands of law enforcement officers to keep communities safe. Example: Kaila Oakley and Niha Jain founded a non-profit medical organization serving community needs. Example: David served as city councilman and helped Mexico City become the first city in Latin America to pass marriage equality, ahead of even San Francisco. The speaker's own story started from a student in a dorm, connecting one community at a time, persisting, and eventually helping connect the whole world. Even global change begins small, with people like us. Whether this generation achieves greater connection and opportunity depends on our ability to build communities and give everyone a sense of purpose.

## 结尾寄语
